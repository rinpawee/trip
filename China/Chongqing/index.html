Jiefangbei CBD
Base Camp
Jiefangbei CBD 解放碑

Stay in the central business district — underground malls, elevated walkways, and street food stalls layered on top of each other. Everything is walking distance. The Liberation Monument stands in the center, surrounded by the city's wildest pedestrian energy.

↗ Google Maps Stay here — best location
Landmark · Evening
Hongya Cave 洪崖洞

The 11-story cliffside building complex that defines Chongqing's image — best experienced at dusk (6:30–7pm) as it lights up neon over the Jialing River. Don't eat inside (overpriced tourist food) — explore and photograph. Then walk the Binjiang Road riverside promenade toward Chaotianmen, the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. At night, bridges lit up and ferries passing below — genuinely cinematic.

↗ Google Maps Go at 6:30pm for the glow
🔥
Liu Yishou Hotpot
Dinner · Must-Do
Liu Yishou Hotpot 刘一手火锅

Local chain beloved by Chongqing residents — not a tourist trap. Order the split pot (half spicy / half mild). Must-orders: beef tripe (毛肚), duck intestines (鸭肠), fatty beef (肥牛), lotus root, tofu skin. The sesame dipping sauce is everything. Hotpot in 35°C heat is a Chongqing tradition — locals believe spicy food makes you sweat and cool down. Lean in.

Eling Park Panorama
Viewpoint · Free
Eling Park 鹅岭公园

Hilltop park where locals do morning exercise with a panoramic view of the whole city. The Liangjiang Overlook here is one of the best free viewpoints in China — both the Yangtze and Jialing rivers visible at once. Early morning light is stunning and crowd-free. Walk down through Eling Zhengdao heritage lane for a slow breakfast coffee at one of the indie cafés.

↗ Google Maps Go by 7am — crowd-free
🚝
Monorail through building
Icon · Unique
Liziba Station — train through a building

Metro Line 2 passes directly through a residential apartment building on floors 6–8. Locals just living their lives while a monorail glides through their windows. Ride it, then cross to the observation platform for the exterior shot. This is Chongqing in miniature — a city so spatially constrained that the impossible became infrastructure.

↗ Google Maps Cross road for best photo angle
🌁
Crystal Link · 250m altitude
Architecture · Views
Raffles City Crystal Link

Eight skyscrapers connected at the top by a horizontal sky bridge — the Crystal Link — at 250 meters above ground. The view looking down at the Yangtze–Jialing confluence is vertigo-inducing in the best way. Rest from the midday heat in the underground Jiefangbei mall network before going up. Evening: find a hole-in-the-wall shop for Xiaomian (小面) — spicy noodles under ¥15, follow with cold Ci Ba (糍粑) from a street vendor.

Private van to Wulong
Transport · 2.5hrs
Drive to Wulong Karst Park 武隆喀斯特

Hire a private van for the group — ~¥600–800 round trip for 6–7 people, far easier than public transit and worth every yuan. Leave by 7am to beat the heat and midday crowds. Wulong is a UNESCO World Heritage Site 2.5 hours from central Chongqing. Bring water, sunscreen, and closed-toe shoes.

↗ Google Maps Van ¥600–800 return
🕳️
Karst sinkholes
UNESCO · Karst
Tiankeng Difeng 天坑地缝

Three massive karst sinkholes the size of stadiums connected by underground rivers and sheer cliff paths. Walk the canyon floor between them. Absolutely otherworldly — the scale is impossible to convey in photos. The canyon walls tower hundreds of meters, carved by rivers over millions of years.

↗ Google Maps
🌉
★ Three Natural Bridges
★ Trip Highlight · UNESCO
Three Natural Bridges 天生三桥

Three colossal natural stone arches stacked in sequence over a gorge — the filming location for Transformers: Age of Extinction and parts of Mission Impossible. The landscape looks genuinely alien. Scale is hard to comprehend until you're standing inside one of the arches, which soar 200+ meters overhead. Allow 2–3 hours for the full walk. The path is well-maintained even in hot weather.

↗ Google Maps 2–3hr walk · proper shoes
💎
Furong Cave — optional
Optional Add-On
Furong Cave 芙蓉洞

Vast illuminated stalactite cave system — add this if energy and time allow. Spectacular formations throughout. Skip if the group is tired after the bridges — the walk is 4–6km total and the heat takes its toll.

Ciqikou Ancient Town
Heritage · Go Early
Ciqikou Ancient Town 磁器口

A Ming Dynasty river-town that survived modernization — stone alleyways, tea houses, temples, and craft vendors. At 7am it's just locals buying breakfast and elderly residents playing chess. By 10am the tourist buses arrive, so go early and be efficient. Must-eat: Mao Xuewang (毛血旺) — offal, tofu, vegetables in blood-red spicy broth. Also try hemp-spiced duck jaw (麻辣鸭脖) from street vendors.

↗ Google Maps Leave before 10am crowds
🏯
Huguang Guild Hall
History · Rarely Crowded
Huguang Guild Hall 湖广会馆

Stunning Qing Dynasty complex (1759) overlooking the Yangtze. Intricate wood carvings, courtyards, and a museum about Chongqing's river migration history. Rarely crowded and genuinely beautiful. Traditional Chinese opera performances are available via booking — the old teahouse setting makes it unforgettable.

↗ Google Maps
🏛️
Three Gorges Museum
Museum · Free · AC
Three Gorges Museum 三峡博物馆

Massive, air-conditioned, free, and excellent — perfect midday escape from the heat. The WWII Chongqing resistance exhibition is especially moving: the city was bombed heavily by Japan while serving as China's wartime capital. Give it 2 hours. Located next to the People's Square.

↗ Google Maps Free entry · 2 hours
✈️
Jiangbei Airport CKG
Depart
Jiangbei Airport 重庆江北机场 · CKG

Metro Line 10 to CKG takes 45 min — fast, cheap, and reliable. For a group with luggage, a Didi van is ¥80–100 and more comfortable. Grab Jiefangbei souvenirs before heading out: hotpot spice bases (火锅底料), Sichuan peppercorn (花椒), local baijiu (白酒), or handmade lacquerware from Ciqikou.

Practical Info

Everything you actually need to know
🚇
Getting Around
Metro covers all key spots. Didi (Chinese Uber) for groups — download and link a card before arrival. Walking is often best — vertical terrain means unexpected views everywhere.
💳
Payment
WeChat Pay or Alipay essential. Most street food and small restaurants don't accept cards. Bring ¥2,000–3,000 per person for 4 days. Ask your bank about adding a card to WeChat Pay beforehand.
🌡️
Summer Heat
35–40°C, very humid. Do all outdoor activities before 11am or after 5pm. Use midday for air-conditioned museums and underground malls. Stay hydrated obsessively.
📱
Apps to Download
Didi (transport), Pleco (dictionary), Google Translate with Chinese offline pack. Most younger locals understand basic pointing + phone translation.
🍽️
Group Dining
For 6–7 people, always book restaurants via hotel concierge or WeChat contact ahead. Walk-ins for large groups at popular spots often wait 1–2 hours.
✈️
Airport (CKG)
Jiangbei Airport is 45 min from downtown via Metro Line 10 — fast, cheap, reliable. Didi van for group with luggage: ¥80–100. Allow 2.5hr buffer.
